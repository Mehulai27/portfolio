Master's Thesis: Transfer Learning for Biomedical Signal Processing

Machine Learning and Data Analytics Lab, FAU (Dr. Emmanuelle Salin, Arijana Bohr M.Sc., Prof. Dr. Bjoern Eskofier)

May 2024 - Nov 2025

The Question: Can we train a model on one person's brain signals and have it work on someone else's, even though we're trying to decode the same cognitive task?

What I Found: Current domain adaptation methods completely fail for this problem. Models trained on other subjects perform no better than random chance, even when everything is implemented correctly. The models appear to learn idiosyncratic patterns specific to each individual rather than task-relevant features.

Why This Matters: Why This Matters: These results are scientifically valuable. They tell us we need fundamentally different approaches, not just better hyperparameters. Future methods need to be engineered specifically for each cognitive task and should follow the underlying physics of how brain signals are actually generated.

Technical Details:

  • Built robust experimental pipeline processing over 1 million EEG samples with careful quality control
  • Implemented multiple state-of-the-art architectures (EEGNet, EEGConformer, ATCNet, Vision Transformers)
  • Trained hundreds of models with systematic ablation studies to ensure findings weren't implementation artifacts
  • Used proper research infrastructure (MLflow, DVC) to ensure reproducibility
